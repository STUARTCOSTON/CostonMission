All About
Birth

We serve an awesome God. He provides for us when we don't ask for it, he protects us without us knowing it, and he blesses us when we don't deserve it. Of his own will he chose us and caused us to be born again, a concept that has become very familiar recently.

The theme of our lives in these last few months has certainly been birth. The birth Jesus, the birth of the New Year, and the birth of our third child.

Each one of these births has had a different impact on our lives and provided us with different experiences and new perspectives. This newsletter will unfold these three events giving you a look into our lives and the impact these events have had on us.

Birth of
Jesus

Like many Christian families, we really try to make Christmas about Jesus and not about presents or Santa. We do open presents, we do enjoy giving gifts to each other, and we do watch movies with Santa in them, but as our kids grow up we want them to know that Jesus is the ultimate giver and he is the reason we are able to give gifts on Christmas at all. Without Jesus becoming flesh and blood on earth we could never recieve forgiveness. Jesus' birth into this world is worth far more than the gingerbread houses we made, the hallmark movies we watched, and the lights on the house. He is God incarnate. Immanuel. God with us.

Birth of a
New Year

John Piper said it best when he said, "For me, the end of a year is like the end of my life. and 11:59 pm on December 31 is like the moment of my death."

Viewing the end of the year in this way allows us to look back over the past 365 days and see what we wished we had done more of and what we wish we had done less of. It gives us an opportunity to face the question, "did I live it well?" It gives us an opportunity to see whether or not we feel like Jesus is going to say, "Well done, good and faithful servant" Matthew 25:21, or "I never knew you; depart from me, you workers of lawlessness." Matthew 7:23

With a new year comes more grace and a new opportunity to repent.

Planting
